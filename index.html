1898 to 1946

American Era

Filipinos initially saw their relationship with the United States as that of two nations joined in a common struggle against Spain. However, the United States later distanced itself from the interests of the Filipino insurgents. Emilio Aguinaldo was unhappy that the United States would not commit to paper a statement of support for Philippine independence. The islands were ceded by Spain to the United States alongside Puerto Rico and Guam as a result of the latter's victory in the Spanish–American War. A compensation of US$20 million was paid to Spain according to the terms of the 1898 Treaty of Paris. Relations deteriorated and tensions heightened as it became clear that the Americans were in the islands to stay.

The First Philippine Constitution

The Philippine Republic, more commonly known as the First Philippine Republic or the Malolos Republic, was a nascent revolutionary government in the Philippines. It was formally established with the proclamation of the Malolos Constitution on January 21, 1899, in Malolos, Bulacan, and endured until the capture of President Emilio Aguinaldo by the American forces on March 23, 1901, in Palanan, Isabela, which effectively dissolved the First Republic.

The First Philippine Republic was established after the Philippine Revolution against the Spanish Empire (1896–1897) and the Spanish–American War between Spain and the United States (1898). Following the American victory at the Battle of Manila Bay, Aguinaldo returned to the Philippines, issued the Philippine Declaration of Independence on June 12, 1898, and established a revolutionary Philippine government. In December 1898, Sovereignty over the Philippines was transferred from Spain to the United States in the 1898 Treaty of Paris, making the United States formally the Philippines colonial power. The Malolos Constitution establishing the First Philippine Republic was proclaimed the following month. The Philippine–American War began in February 1899, which the Philippine Republic lost.

The Philippine Republic was the first constitutional republic in Asia. Although there were several Asian republics predating the First Philippine Republic for example, the Mahajanapadas of ancient India, the Lanfang Republic, the Republic of Formosa or the Republic of Ezo, the Republic at Malolos was the first to frame a comprehensive constitution duly approved by a partially elected congress.

Japanese Era

On January 2, 1942 is the date when the Japanese took over Manila and later on formed Japanese Military Administration to replace the government of the exiled Commonwealth. It used the current structure of the administration that is already in placed and they coerced it to establish a government high ranking Commonwealth officials that is left behind to form a government in order to win the people's support, Japanese Prime Minister Hideki Tojo promised the Filipinos independence than the Tydings-Mcduffie Act had scheduled.

A committee appointed by the Philippine Executive Commission, the body set up by the Japanese to rule the Philippines in place of the Commonwealth of the Philippines that had formed a government-in-exile, drafted the 1943 Constitution. In the middle of 1942, The new Charter was ratified in 1943 by an assembly of named provincial members of Kalibapi, an organisation set up by the Japanese to replace all previous political parties, upon the approval of the draft by the Committee. The Second Republic was officially declared (1943-1945) upon ratification by the Kalibapi assembly. On October 14, 1943, José P. Laurel was elected President by the National Assembly and sworn into office. Strong executive powers were provided for by the 1943 Constitution. The Legislature consisted of a unicameral National Assembly and only those deemed to be anti-US were allowed to stand for election, while most members were appointed rather than elected in practice.

The 1943 Constitution - Preamble

The Filipino people, imploring the aid of Divine Providence and desiring to lead a free national existence, do hereby proclaim their independence, and in order to establish a government that shall promote the general welfare, conserve and develop the patrimony of the Nation, and contribute to the creation of a world order based on peace, liberty, and moral justice, do ordain this Constitution.

The 1943 Constitution

According to the article the 1943 Constitution of the Republic of the Philippines, composed of a preamble and twelve articles, creates a Republican state with a powerful executive branch and subordinate legislative also judicial branches. The executive power is vested in the President, who is to be elected by the members of the National Assembly from among themselves. A limited legislative power is exercised by the unicameral National Assembly whose members, like the President, are not directly elected by the people. Rather, the Assembly is to be composed of representatives from each province elected in Kalibapi conventions throughout the country with appointed governors and mayors as ex-officio members.The 1943 Constitution enumerates the duties and rights of the citizens, requires the government to develop Tagalog as the national language, and stipulates that one year after the termination of the Great East Asia War or the World War II; a new constitution shall be formulated and adopted to replace this Constitution

1946

After World War II

After the liberation of the Philippines from the Imperial Japan, the 1935 Constitution came back into effect after it was nullified by the Japanese-sponsored government in favor of the 1943 Philippine Constitution. In 1947, the 1935 Constitution was altered, giving United States citizens equal rights with the Filipino citizens to develop natural resources and operate public utilities in the country. The 1935 Constitution was used until September 23, 1972, the time when then-President Ferdinand Marcos declared Martial Law to the Philippines

1973

1973 Philippine constitution

Before the declaration of the Martial Law, a Constitutional Convention was already in process of changing the 1935 Constitution, the then-current constitution, however the time of the declaration of the Martial Law, the Convention moved quickly and within two months after the declaration, the draft was approved and was presented to Marcos. President Marcos then issued the Presidential Decree No. 86 which instituted the “Citizen’s Assemblies” in order to ratify the newly drafted constitution in means of a Viva Voce or oral voting, the assembly ran from January 10 – 17, 1973. On January, 17, 1973, President Marco issued the Proclamation No. 1102 which proclaimed that the new constitution was officially ratified by the Filipino people and thereby was in effect.

The 1973 Constitution provides the shift from presidential to parliamentary system of government. President Marcos abolish the congress in favor of the National Assembly for legislative power. A Prime Minister is elected among the National Assembly which serves as the commander-in-chief of the Philippine Armed Forces and the head of the government. The President is elected among the members of the National Assembly and serves as the symbolic head of state with a six-year term with unlimited number of terms. The judicial power is vested in the Supreme Court which composed of a Chief Justice and 14 justices, which was appointed by the Prime Minister, along with the judges of the lower courts. The Constitution retains the independence of the Commission of Elections (COMELEC) and also creating the Civil Service Commission (CSC), the Commission on Audit (COA), three of which composes of the current constitutional bodies, as well as the National Economic Development Authority (NEDA). The Constitution was amended four times:

  • The 1976 amendment provided: the Interim Batasang Pambansa, replacing the Interim National Assembly; and that the President would become the Prime Minister and continue to exercise legislative powers until the Martial Law was lifted.
  • The 1980 amendment extended the retirement age of the judiciary to 70 years.
  • The 1981 amendment replaced the parliamentary system to a semi-president system and provided: The President restored its executive powers. The direct election of the President was restored. The Executive committee, composed of the Prime Minister and 14 members, was created to “assist the President in the exercise of his powers and functions and in the performance of his duties as he may prescribe”; and The Prime Minister was a mere head of the Cabinet. for electoral reforms; and The transfer of private lands to natural-born citizens of the Philippines that lost their citizenship for residential use.
  • The 1984 amendment abolished the Executive Committee in favor of the restoration of the Vice-President position.

The 1973 Constitution was used until the 1986 People Power Revolution on February 25, 1986 when Ferdinand Marcos was overthrown as President of the Philippines.

1986

1986 Constitution

Also known as the “Freedom Constitution”, it was issued as Proclamation No. 3 by then-President Corazon Aquino. provisions of the constitution were adopted from the 1973 Constitution which President Aquino also abolished some of the other provisions.

The Constitution highlights the following

  • The president cannot abolish the Congress.
  • A new constitution will be drafted sixty (60) days after the proclamation of the constitution.

Before the creation of the “Freedom Constitution”, President Aquino was given three options: To revert to the 1935 Constitution, to retain the 1973 constitution, which she rejected, or create a new one. The constitution also serves as a provisional, or transitional, constitution which only lasted for only a year before using the 1987 Constitution, the current constitution being used in the country.

1987

The 1987 Constitution

Sixty (60) days after the proclamation of the 1986 Freedom Constitution, the Constitution Committee, composed of forty-eight (48) members from different backgrounds, began drafting for a permanent constitution. Several issues arose from the committee such as the form of government system to adopt, the abolition of death penalty, the retention of U.S. bases from Clark and Subic, and the integration of the economic policies to the constitution. Three of the delegates, one of them is Lino Brocka, a film director, resigned before the completion of the constitution. The draft was finished on October 12, 1986 and was ratified after a national plebiscite was conducted on February 2, 1987, which was celebrated as the “Constitution Day” in the Philippines. President Aquino then issued Proclamation No. 58, thus implementing the 1987 Constitution.

The Constitution highlights the following:

  • The presidential term is a single one in six (6) years.
  • Provided the three (3) government branches, namely: The Executive, The Legislative, and The Judicial branch.
  • The president may declare martial law for only 60 days, the congress may reject the declaration or extend it.
  • The removal of the office of the Prime Minister and the Batasang Pambansa, the unicameral legislation.
  • Multi-party elections are now being held.
